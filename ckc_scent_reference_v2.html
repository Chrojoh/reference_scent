🐾 9.5.13
Minor Faults
One (1) minor fault permitted per search area and still qualify
1 allowed / search
△Dropped toy or food reward in the search area
△Not crossing the designated start line
△Handler cues the dog, or has the dog continue to search to pinpoint the hide location, when the judge asks "Where?"
△Moving a container / object / distractor more than a few inches (by handler or dog, with any part of body)
△Safety issues — e.g. taking dog off leash in an on-leash search, placing dog in an unsafe situation
🐾 9.5.13
Non-Qualifying Faults
Any NQ fault = non-qualifying performance for that search area
Immediate NQ
✗Damage to search area due to excessive pawing, digging, or scratching
✗Picking up or biting a container/object (dog)
✗Handler calls "Alert" on the incorrect location
✗Handler calls "Finish" before all hides in the area have been located
✗Maximum time for the class or search area is exceeded
✗Handler is not able to point to the location of the hide when asked by the judge
✗Any harsh handling or harsh corrections by the handler
✗Dog disrupts the search area unduly — intentional biting, hard scratching, pouncing (note: dog's nose or feet simply getting caught on containers is not a fault)
✗Safety issues — e.g. taking dog off leash in an on-leash search
✗Excellent / Master Interior & Exterior: Failing to call "Finish" or "Clear" per search area before that area's time expires
✗Master Containers: Failing to call "Finish" or "Clear" before maximum course time elapses
🐾 9.5
Handler Rules During the Search
› Say "Alert" to confirm each find. If verbal confirmation is not possible (environmental factors or disability), inform the judge of an alternate confirmation before the search begins — e.g. an obvious hand signal.
› "Finish" / "Clear" — used in Master class (all components) and Excellent/Master Interior and Exterior. Must be called when handler believes all hides in the area have been found. Stops the clock for that area. Failure to call before time = NQ.
› Handlers may touch the dog at any time during the search for direction or praise.
› Handlers may not touch objects in the search area without express permission from the judge.
› After a correct find, handler may reward with food or toy. Toy must not be thrown or make noise. Food/toys cannot be used to bait the dog before or during the search.
› Handlers and spectators may not discuss the search, hide locations, or performance until all teams in the class have run.
› Handlers may not watch other exhibitors from their own class prior to their own turn.
› No person outside the search area may assist the handler or disclose hide locations. Spectators who give aid may be asked to leave by the judge.
🐾 9.4
Equipment Rules
✓ Allowed: Plain, pliable collar or harness approved by the judge. Martingale collars. Treat pouches or fanny packs to carry food rewards. Paw covers, booties, jackets, sweaters, hair ties (judge has final say).
✗ Not Allowed: Electronic collars, prong collars, head halters, choke chains, no-pull harnesses, belly bands, no-bark collars (including those that spray citronella or DAP calming collars).
› The final word on whether an accessory is allowed rests with the judge.
🐾 9.9 — Distractions
Distraction Rules
Toy Distractions
›Must not contain any artificial scent such as another essential oil, perfume, or scented lotion.
›In interior and exterior searches, toy distractions may be hidden or visible.
Food Distractions
›May be any kind of dog food or human food (except foods harmful to dogs).
›Must be enclosed in a container so the searching dog cannot consume the food or the container.
Human Distractions
›A person other than the judge who is present inside the search area.
›May be standing or sitting. May look at the dog but must not maintain eye contact if the dog's attention turns to them. May not speak to or intentionally touch the dog.
🐾 10.1 — Class Titles

Class titles are earned by getting one (1) qualifying score in each of the three components (Containers, Interior, Exterior) at that class level. Qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial. All titles are suffix titles.

SDIN
Scent Detection Instinct
Optional entry-level title — Containers component only
Optional
Prerequisite None. Open to dogs who have not yet attained an SDN title.
Requirement One (1) qualifying score in the Instinct class (Containers component only).
Components Containers only — no Interior or Exterior at Instinct level.
Notes This is an optional, standalone title. Earning SDIN is not required before progressing to Novice.
SDN
Scent Detection Novice
First full class title — all three Novice components required
Prerequisite None. Open to all dogs who have not yet attained an SDO title.
Requirement One (1) qualifying score in each of: Novice Containers + Novice Interior + Novice Exterior.
Same trial? No — qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial.
SDO
Scent Detection Open
All three Open components required
Prerequisite Must first obtain SDN (Scent Detection Novice). Open to dogs who have not yet attained an SDE title.
Requirement One (1) qualifying score in each of: Open Containers + Open Interior + Open Exterior.
Same trial? No — qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial.
SDE
Scent Detection Excellent
All three Excellent components required
Prerequisite Must first obtain SDO. Open to dogs who have not yet attained an SDM title.
Requirement One (1) qualifying score in each of: Excellent Containers + Excellent Interior + Excellent Exterior.
Same trial? No — qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial.
SDM
Scent Detection Master
All three Master components required
Prerequisite Must first obtain SDE.
Requirement One (1) qualifying score in each of: Master Containers + Master Interior + Master Exterior.
Same trial? No — qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial.
SDGM
Scent Detection Grand Master
The highest class title — five additional Qs in every Master component
Post-SDM
Prerequisite Must have already obtained SDM.
Requirement Five (5) additional qualifying scores in the Master class in each of the three components — Master Containers, Master Interior, and Master Exterior.
Same trial? No — qualifying scores do not have to be earned at the same trial.
🐾 10.1.7–10.1.9 — Component Titles

Component titles are earned independently by progressing through all four levels (Novice → Open → Excellent → Master) in a single component. These are earned separately from the class titles above. A dog may not be entered at a component level for which they are not yet eligible. Qualifying scores can be from separate trials. All are suffix titles.

SDC
Scent Detection Container
🐾 10.1.7
Requirement One (1) qualifying score at each level in the Container component: Novice + Open + Excellent + Master.
Order Must be completed in order: Novice → Open → Excellent → Master.
SDI
Scent Detection Interior
🐾 10.1.8
Requirement One (1) qualifying score at each level in the Interior component: Novice + Open + Excellent + Master.
Order Must be completed in order: Novice → Open → Excellent → Master.
SDX
Scent Detection Exterior
🐾 10.1.9
Requirement One (1) qualifying score at each level in the Exterior component: Novice + Open + Excellent + Master.
Order Must be completed in order: Novice → Open → Excellent → Master.
🐾 10.1.11 — Once a dog has qualified in a component at one class level, they may move to the same component at the next class level. Example: a dog that qualifies in Novice Containers may move to Open Containers.
